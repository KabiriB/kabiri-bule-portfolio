Thinking in Data, Systems, and Behaviour

This blog is where I think aloud about data, decisions, and the systems that sit beneath everyday numbers. Some posts are about dashboards and tools, but the real focus is on how organisations make sense of the world: what they see, what they miss, and how that shapes the choices they make.

I write as a practitioner who moves between analytics, research, and strategy. That means paying attention not just to visualisations and KPIs, but also to uncertainty, bias, behaviour, and context. The aim is always the same: to move from noise to clarity, and from clarity to better decisions.

What I Write About

  • Data & decision-making – when data helps, when it misleads, and how to stay critical.
  • Visualisation & dashboards – beyond pretty charts to actually useful tools.
  • Behaviour & systems – why people do what they do inside complex organisations.
  • Development & cities – how data shapes the way we think about African cities and futures.
